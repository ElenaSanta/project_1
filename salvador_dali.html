Salvador Dali

Salvador Dalí was one of the most versatile and prolific painters of
the 20th century. Although he is best remembered for his painting,
during his long career he also dabbled in sculpture, printmaking,
fashion, advertising, writing, and, perhaps most famously, filmmaking,
collaborating with Luis Bunuel and Alfred Hitchcock. Dalí was known for
his flamboyant personality and unquestionable technical mastery. In his
early use of organic morphology, his works bear the hallmarks of his
Spanish contemporaries Pablo Picasso and Joan Miró. His paintings also
demonstrate a fascination with Classical and Renaissance art, evident
in the surrealist style and religious symbolism of his later works. Dalí
is often associated with the Surrealist movement, despite his official
expulsion from the group in 1934 for his reactionary political views.
